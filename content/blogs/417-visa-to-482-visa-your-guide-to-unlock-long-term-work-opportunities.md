## 417 Visa to 482 Visa: Your Guide to Unlock Long-Term Work Opportunities

You are currently in Australia on a 417 visa, and now you love working here, gaining valuable experience, and building your life. But this visa is not permanent; it is temporary and comes with many limitations on work and stay in Australia.

Here, one stronger and more stable option which peeped out for your stay is the Employer-sponsored 482 Visa.

This allows you to work full-time, stay longer, and even enhances your chances of getting an Australia PR. So, in this guide, we will understand the complete process of how one can move from a Subclass 417 visa to a Subclass 482 visa, including eligibility requirements, key benefits, and a step-by-step transition process.

Let’s get started…



## Overview of Moving from a 417 Visa to a 482 Visa

- Hold a valid 417 Working Holiday Visa  
- Secure employment in an eligible occupation with an approved (or willing to be approved) employer  
- Employer lodges Standard Business Sponsorship (SBS) and Nomination  
- You apply for the 482 Skills in Demand visa  
- Once granted you can work full-time with approved sponsor, stay up to 4 years and also apply for PR via subclass 186  



## 417 vs 482 Visa: Understanding the Major Differences

| Feature | 417 Working Holiday | 482 Skills in Demand |
|--------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Validity | Up to 12 months | Up to 4 years |
| Same Employer Limit | 6 months (exemptions apply) | No limit |
| Sponsorship Requirement | No employer sponsorship required | Requires sponsorship from an approved Australian employer |
| PR Pathway | Not direct | Yes, via subclass 186 |
| Age limit | 18 to 30 or 35 years (depending on country) | No strict age limit |
| Family Members | Cannot include partner or dependent | Can include partner and dependent |
| Min. Salary Threshold | Not applicable | AUD 76,515 (TSMIT 2024) |



## Why Transition from 417 Visa to 482 Visa?

Moving from a Subclass 417 to a Subclass 482 visa offers many benefits. Here are some:

- No more limit to work with the same employer for only 6 months  
- You can stay longer in Australia (generally 4 years) to build a long-term career  
- Most of the jobs associated with 482 visas are skilled positions, which means you can get the benefit of a higher salary and professional development  
- One of the biggest advantages that comes with a 482 visa is the pathway to Australia permanent residency. (After working with your sponsoring employer, meeting basic salary requirements, you may qualify for the Subclass 186 Employer Nomination Scheme visa.)  



## What are the Eligibility Requirements to Move From a 417 visa to a 482 visa?

To transition from a 417 visa to a 482 visa, you must meet specific eligibility requirements. You must have:

- Employer Sponsorship: You need an Australian employer who is ready to sponsor you for a skilled role.  
- Relevant work Experience: The 482 visa typically requires at least 1 year (and sometimes 2 years) of relevant work experience in your nominated occupation or a closely related field.  
- Nomination Approval from Employer Side: Your employer must submit a sponsorship and nomination application.  
- English Language Requirement: Meet the English language proficiency requirement.  


## How to Transition from a 417 Visa to 482 Visa?

Step 1: Find an Employer Willing to Sponsor You  

Step 2: Check whether your occupation is eligible based on an employer-sponsored visa  

Step 3: Your employer will submit the sponsorship and nomination application  

Step 4: Once the nomination is approved, you can apply for a 482 visa  

However, note this thing that, if your 417 visa expires while your 482 visa is being processed, you may receive a bridging visa.

**Growmore Tip:** Never wait until the last 3 months of your 417 visa. Sponsorship processes may take time. So, start the conversation with your employer at least 6 months before your visa expires.

## FAQs

**Can I apply for a Subclass 482 visa while on a Subclass 417 visa?**

Yes, you can apply onshore if you meet the eligibility requirements and have an employer willing to sponsor you.

**How long can I stay in Australia on a 482 visa?**

You can stay and work in Australia for up to 4 years, depending on your occupation and visa stream.

**What happens if my 417 visa expires while my 482 visa is processing?**

You will usually receive a bridging visa, allowing you to stay in Australia lawfully.

**Can I apply for a 482 visa without work experience?**

No, you usually need at least 1 year of relevant work experience in your nominated occupation.

**When should I start the transition from a 417 visa to a 482 visa?**

Start at least 6 months before your 417 visa expires to allow enough time for sponsorship and processing.
