The Temporary Skill Shortage visa (Subclass 482) is one of the most commonly used employer-sponsored pathways for skilled professionals to work in Australia. However, to get approval for this visa, it is important to avoid common mistakes made by both employers and applicants.

In most cases, refusals are not due to ineligibility but result from weak documentation, poor role justification, or non-compliance with visa requirements. Here, the core issue is that many applications fail to align with how the Department actually assesses evidence and sponsorship requirements.

As a result, even genuine cases face refusal due to gaps in preparation and understanding. This blog breaks down what you need to get right to avoid costly mistakes.

## **Mistakes Applicants Make**

### **1.  Choosing the Wrong Occupation or Mismatched Duties**
\
Sometimes, applicants select a profession based on visa eligibility, rather than job fit. Their duties in their current role do not match the nominated ANZSCO occupation. For example, an applicant working as a Software tester would choose Analyst Programmer (ANZSCO 261311) even though their day-to-day tasks are testing, debugging and quality assurance. This type of mismatch is one of the fastest ways to set off a refusal.

**How to Avoid it:**

* Make sure your role aligns with at least the majority of ANZSCO-listed duties
* Do not only rely on job titles, but also focus on actual tasks performed
* Cross-check your duties with what the employer is nominating

### **2. Weak or Generic Work Experience Documents**
\
Generic experience letters without detailed responsibilities or proof reduce credibility. If your documents don’t clearly show what you did and where, the case officer may question the authenticity of your experience.

**How to Avoid it:**

* Instead of providing generic HR templates, submit detailed duty-based reference letters
* Include reporting lines, tools used, and scope of work
* Ensure documents are on official letterhead with verifiable details

### **3. Incorrect or Expired Skills Assessment**
\
Using the wrong assessing authority or an expired assessment can make your application invalid. Many applicants assume their qualifications are enough without verifying formal assessment requirements. 

**How to Avoid it:**

* Always confirm if your occupation requires a skills assessment and which authority is relevant
* Check that your qualifications and work experience meet the assessing body’s criteria
* Make sure the assessment is valid at the time of application lodgement

### **4. Ignoring Health, Character, or Visa History**
\
Non-disclosure of past visa refusals or delays in medical and police checks can create serious issues. Incomplete or inconsistent information raises integrity concerns.

**How to Avoid it:**

* Disclose all previous visa refusals, cancellations, and immigration history upfront
* Complete medicals and police checks on time and ensure there are no gaps
* If there are any past issues, address them proactively with a proper explanation instead of hiding them

## Mistakes Employers Make

### **1. Not Proving the Role Is Genuine**
\
If the Department gets the sense that the role has been created solely for the purpose of sponsoring a visa, the nomination will most likely be refused. Employers have to be able to show clearly that the job is real, that it is needed, and that it is actually part of their day-to-day business.

**How to Avoid it:**

* Clearly demonstrate how the role fits into your business operations and why it is needed
* Provide organisational charts, business activity details, and workload evidence
* Ensure the duties align with business size, structure, and industry

### **2. Selecting the Wrong Occupation for the Role**
\
Choosing an occupation that “sounds right” but doesn’t match actual duties is a common mistake. This creates misalignment between the role and visa requirements.

**How to Avoid it:**

* Compare the role responsibilities directly against ANZSCO descriptions
* Avoid inflating or modifying duties just to meet eligibility
* Keep consistency between job description, contract, and nomination documents

### **3. Labour Market Testing (LMT) Done Incorrectly**
\
[Labour Market Testing](https://www.growmore.one/our-services/employers/labour-market-testing-lmt-for-sc494-and-sc482-visas/) is a strict requirement, and even small mistakes like missing details, incorrect wording, or expired advertisements can lead to refusal. Because the Department expects clear evidence that genuine efforts were made to hire an Australian worker before offering the role to a visa applicant.

**How to Avoid it:**

* Ensure ads meet all requirements (correct duration, platform, and mandatory details like salary range if applicable)
* Keep evidence of all advertisements, including screenshots and invoices
* Make sure the role advertised matches the nominated position exactly

### **4. Salary Below Market or Compliance Standards**
\
If the offered salary falls below TSMIT or doesn’t match the market rate, it raises immediate red flags. Here, even small inconsistencies between contracts, payroll, or job details can lead to refusal.

**How to Avoid it:**

* Benchmark the salary against market data and ensure it meets or exceeds TSMIT
* Include all guaranteed earnings correctly without any type of exaggerated figures
* Be prepared to justify how the salary reflects the role, location, and experience level

## FAQs

### **1. Do I always need a skills assessment?**

Not in every case, but many occupations do require one. The problem is people assume they don’t need it or use the wrong authority. If it’s required and missing or invalid, your application won’t even be considered properly.

### **2. How strict is Labour Market Testing (LMT)?**

Stricter than most people think. It’s not just about posting a job ad, but everything from timing to wording has to meet requirements. Even small mistakes can lead to refusal.

### **3. What matters more: job title or actual duties?**

Duties matter more. You can call yourself anything, but if your day-to-day work doesn’t match the ANZSCO occupation, it raises immediate concerns.

### **4. Can a lower salary be justified if the candidate has less experience?**

Only to a certain extent. The salary still needs to meet TSMIT and reflect the market rate. If it looks underpaid, the application will likely be questioned.

### **5. Can I reapply if my 482 visa gets refused?**

Yes, but don’t expect a reset. The Department will look at your previous refusal. If you haven’t fixed what went wrong, you’re likely to face the same outcome again.
