## Australia PR 2026: Eligibility, Process & In-Demand Jobs

Whether you are already **living in Australia on a student/work visa** or **still overseas planning your move**, securing Permanent Residency (PR) is the key to long-term stability and career growth.

However, obtaining PR is not an automatic or simple process. It requires strategic planning that aligns with the right eligibility profile, a competitive points score, and a clear understanding of the occupations in demand for 2026.

In this guide, we break down the eligibility criteria, the step-by-step visa process, and the most in-demand jobs to help you plan your pathway effectively.

## Australia PR Eligibility Criteria

To qualify for skilled migration, you must meet the following mandatory requirements:

1.  **Age:** Must be under 45 years at the time of invitation.
2.  **English Proficiency:** At least "Competent" English (IELTS, PTE, OET, or equivalent).
3.  **Eligible Occupation:** Your role must be on the relevant **Skilled Occupation List**.
4.  **Skills Assessment:** A positive result from the designated assessing authority.
5.  **Points Requirement:** Minimum **65 points** (higher scores improve selection chances).
6.  **Health & Character:** Must pass medical exams and provide police clearances.
7.  **Expression of Interest (EOI):** Submitted via SkillSelect to receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA).

## Available Pathways to Australia PR

* **Skilled Independent (Subclass 189):** Points-tested; no sponsorship required.
* **Skilled Nominated (Subclass 190):** Requires nomination by an Australian state or territory.
* **Skilled Work Regional (Subclass 491):** Regional visa leading to PR (Subclass 191).
* **Employer-Sponsored (Subclass 186):** Requires a nomination from an Australian employer.
* **Family & Partner:** Visas for partners (820/801) or eligible parents of citizens/PR holders.

## Step-by-Step Australia PR Visa Process

1.  **Check Eligibility:** Confirm your occupation is in demand and calculate your points.
2.  **Skills Assessment:** Get your credentials verified by the relevant authority.
3.  **English Test:** Achieve the required score in IELTS, PTE, or equivalent.
4.  **Submit EOI:** Submit your Expression of Interest via **SkillSelect**.
5.  **Receive ITA:** Wait for an Invitation to Apply based on your ranking.
6.  **Submit Application:** Upload all documents and pay fees within the deadline.
7.  **Visa Grant:** Receive your decision and prepare for relocation.

## FAQS

**Can I apply for Australia PR from outside Australia?**

Yes, both onshore and offshore candidates can apply. Offshore applicants must complete their skills assessment and English testing before submitting an EOI.

**Which visa is best for skilled migration?**

It depends on your profile. Subclass 189 is ideal for high scorers, while 190 and 491 are excellent for those seeking state support.

**Is IELTS mandatory?**

No. You can use other approved tests like PTE, OET, or Cambridge (CAE).

**How long does the process take in 2026?**

Processing times vary by subclass and document accuracy. Ensuring a complete and error-free application is the best way to avoid delays.
