## General Skilled Migration (GSM) – Improvements Observed

Processing times for GSM visas have improved significantly across key subclasses.

* Subclass 189 has reduced from approximately 12 months to 6 months.
* Subclass 190 has reduced from approximately 25 months to 16 months.
* Subclass 191 has reduced from approximately 16 months to 8 months.

This reflects a continued policy focus on skilled migration to address labour shortages in Australia. The reduction in processing times suggests prioritisation within this stream.


## Employer-Sponsored Visas – Ongoing Pressure

Employer-sponsored visa categories continue to show mixed outcomes.

* Subclass 186 Direct Entry has improved from approximately 20 months to 15 months.
* Subclass 482 (Skills in Demand) has experienced a slight increase from approximately 7 months to 8 months under the Core stream.

Despite some improvement in permanent employer-sponsored pathways, temporary sponsorship pathways remain impacted by demand and processing backlogs.

## Partner Visas – Limited Movement

* Subclass 100 (offshore partner visa) has seen a modest reduction of approximately 2 to 3 months.
* Onshore partner visas (Subclass 820/801) show no significant change.

While there is minor improvement in offshore processing, partner visa timelines remain relatively extended overall.

## Other Visa Categories

* Subclass 407 (Training Visa) has shown a reduction trend compared to previous periods.
* Subclass 300 and 309 have experienced increased processing times.
* Regional and provisional pathways such as Subclass 491 and 494 also show extended processing periods.


## Practical Implications

* Applicants considering GSM pathways may benefit from faster processing trends.
* Employer-sponsored applicants should continue to plan for longer timelines, particularly under temporary streams.
* Partner visa applicants should maintain realistic expectations despite minor improvements.

## Important Note

### **The above processing times:**

* Represent general averages (50% and 90% processing benchmarks)

* Do not guarantee processing timeframes for individual cases

* Case complexity

* Departmental priorities
* Individual circumstances
