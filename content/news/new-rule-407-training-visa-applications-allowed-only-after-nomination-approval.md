## New Rule: 407 Training Visa Applications Allowed Only After Nomination Approval

If you are planning to apply for the **Subclass 407 Training Visa**, you need to be aware of an important rule change effective **March 11, 2026**.

Australia is tightening the application process. Applicants will no longer be allowed to submit a visa application until nomination approval is granted.


## Major Change to the 407 Visa Application Process

Under the updated regulation, applicants can no longer lodge sponsorship, nomination, and visa applications at the same time.

### Previously, applicants could submit:

- Temporary Activities Sponsor application  
- Nomination for the training program  
- Subclass 407 visa application  

### New step-by-step process:

1. Employer must be approved as a sponsor  
2. Nomination for the training program must be approved  
3. Only then can the 407 visa application be lodged  

This change mainly affects the structured workplace-based training stream, often used by graduates to gain work experience or extend their stay while planning for **Australia PR**.


## Reason Behind the Rule Tightening

According to the Department of Home Affairs:

- There has been a surge in 407 visa applications since mid-2024  
- Some applicants were using the visa to extend their stay instead of genuine training  
- Many nomination applications failed to meet requirements  
- Processing delays increased for genuine applicants  

The new rule ensures that training programs are properly assessed before visa applications are submitted.


## Expert Advice for 407 Visa Applicants

- **Apply early if possible:** Current system still allows combined applications  
- **Confirm sponsor eligibility early:** Employers must qualify as sponsors first  
- **Start early if on a temporary visa:** Nomination approval may take up to 10 months  
- **Ensure training is genuine:** Program must clearly show structured workplace training
