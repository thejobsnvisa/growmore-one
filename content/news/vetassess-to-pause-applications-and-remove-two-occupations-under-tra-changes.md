## VETASSESS to Pause Applications and Remove Two Occupations Under TRA Changes

Trade professionals planning to submit their Australian skills assessment through VETASSESS should take note of an important update.

Due to changes introduced by Trades Recognition Australia (TRA), there will be a temporary pause on certain applications, and two occupations will be removed from the assessment process handled by VETASSESS.

These changes will affect applicants using the Offshore Skills Assessment Program (OSAP) and the Temporary Skill Shortage Skills Assessment Program (TSS).


## Two Occupations Removed from VETASSESS Assessments

One major change from **30 March 2026** is that VETASSESS will stop accepting new applications for:

- Fitter-Welder *(ANZSCO 323213)*  
- Pastrycook *(ANZSCO 351112)*  

Applicants working in these trades may need to look for alternative assessment arrangements depending on further guidance issued by TRA.


## New Assessment Fees Coming

TRA has confirmed that new assessment fees will apply to applications submitted from **30 March 2026 onwards**.

The updated fees will be officially published on **23 March 2026** in the trade occupations fee schedule, which includes assessments conducted by VETASSESS.

Applicants planning to lodge their skills assessment later this month should review the updated fee structure to ensure there is no impact on their **Australia PR pathway**.



## Temporary Pause on Stage 1 Applications

To implement the new updates, VETASSESS has confirmed a temporary pause on Stage 1 Documentary Evidence Assessment applications.

### Important Dates:

- From **9:00 AM (AEDT) on 13 March 2026 to 9:00 AM (AEDT) on 30 March 2026**  
  → No new Stage 1 applications will be accepted  

- From **9:00 AM (AEDT) on 13 March 2026 to 9:00 AM (AEDT) on 23 March 2026**  
  → Online portal will remain closed  


Applicants who submitted and paid before **13 March 2026** will not be affected, and their applications will continue processing normally.
