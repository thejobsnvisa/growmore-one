## What Is Parent Visa 804?

The **Subclass 804**, also known as the **Aged Parent Visa**, allows eligible parents to live in Australia permanently.

This visa is specifically designed for parents who:

- Meet Australia’s age pension requirements  
- Are already in Australia at the time of application  

Working with **Australian Immigration Consultants** or a registered **Immigration Agent** can help ensure your application meets all legal requirements.

## Eligibility Criteria

To apply for the **Parent Visa 804**, applicants must:

- Be **67 years or older**  
- Have a child who is an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible New Zealand citizen  
- Meet the **Balance of Family Test**  
- Pass health and character requirements  
- Provide an **Assurance of Support** (financial backing)  

## Benefits of Parent Visa 804

- ✔ Permanent residency in Australia  
- ✔ Access to **Medicare** healthcare services  
- ✔ Pathway to Australian citizenship  
- ✔ Travel freely in and out of Australia  
- ✔ Live close to children and grandchildren  
- ✔ Stay in Australia on a **Bridging Visa** while the application is processed  
- ✔ Travel overseas with a **Bridging Visa B**  
- ✔ Work full-time on **Bridging Visa A or B**  

## Processing Time – Important Update

One of the biggest challenges of the **Parent Visa 804** is the extremely long waiting period.

⏳ Due to limited visa allocations, processing times can exceed **30 years**.

Because of this, many families consult a **Visa Agent Australia** or registered **Immigration Agent** to explore faster alternatives.

## Alternative Visa Options

If you are looking for quicker pathways, consider:

- **Contributory Parent Visa**  
  - Higher cost  
  - Significantly faster processing  

- **Sponsored Parent Temporary Visa**  
  - Faster approval  
  - Does not provide permanent residency  


## Final Thoughts

The **Parent Visa 804** is a valuable option for families seeking long-term reunification in Australia.

However, due to long processing times, careful planning and professional guidance are highly recommended.
