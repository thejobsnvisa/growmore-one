## When the Client First Approached Us

The client was originally from Pakistan, but at the time when he approached us, he was already in Australia on a Subclass 476 visa and working in Western Australia as an Energy and Environmental Engineer. Like many temporary visa holders, he was also facing the same uncertainty about how to secure permanent residency before his temporary visa expires.

Our Registered Migration Agent (RMA) did a consultation with him and conducted a detailed profile evaluation. Our team reviews his educational background, offshore & Australian work experience, current employment in Western Australia, and the employer details who were willing to sponsor.

After a thorough assessment, our RMA identified that the client had more than the required 3 years of relevant experience, which makes him a strong candidate for the Subclass 186 Direct Entry stream.

Another positive factor in this case was that the client had already secured an employer on his own, which significantly strengthened the case and allowed us to move forward with this visa subclass.

## Identifying the Right Pathway

As Subclass 186 has three available streams: Direct Entry Stream, Labour Agreement Stream, and Temporary Residence Transition Stream, the next step was to determine which pathway would best suit the client's situation.

After assessing the available options and the employer's condition, our RMA identified that the Direct Entry Stream was the most appropriate pathway. So, we proceed with further processing in this pathway.

## Moving Forward with Skills Assessment

Once the pathway was confirmed, the next important step was the skills assessment, which is mandatory for Subclass 186 Direct Entry applications.

Since the client was working as an Environmental Engineer, we proceeded with Engineers Australia for the occupation:

Environmental Engineer - ANZSCO 233915

This stage required careful preparation, as Engineers Australia has strict assessment requirements, particularly for employment claims and the relevance of experience. Our team managed the entire process, including:

- Detailed review of qualification documents
- Verifying employment history and role responsibilities
- Aligning work experience with the Environmental Engineer occupation requirements
- Preparing supporting documentation and evidence

As a result, the client successfully received a positive skills assessment, and we moved forward to the employer nomination and Subclass 186 visa application stage.

## Lodging the Nomination and Visa Application

After preparing all required documentation and coordinating closely with the employer, we lodged both the nomination and visa application on 19 June 2025.

However, during processing, the Department requested additional information for both nomination and visa applications.

### Nomination RFI

Apart from submitting a detailed nomination application, the Department requested additional information (RFI) to further assess the employer's eligibility and the genuineness of the nominated position.

**The Department specifically requested:**

- Evidence of the employer's financial capability and
- Confirmation of the genuine need for the nominated position

At this stage, our Registered Migration Agent worked closely with the employer and provided clear guidance on preparing the required documentation, including:

- Providing company financial documents to demonstrate business stability
- Drafting a detailed position description aligned with business operations
- Demonstrating genuine operational need for the role
- Showing that the position existed within the business and was not created solely for sponsorship

With properly structured documentation and a well-prepared response, the nomination requirements were successfully addressed.

### Visa Application RFI

For the visa application, the Department requested a polio vaccination certificate for our client.

Our team guided the client regarding this and ensured timely submission to avoid delays.

## FAQs

**1. Can I apply for Subclass 186 while already in Australia?**

Yes. Many applicants transition from temporary visas to Subclass 186 while working in Australia. If you have a suitable employer and meet eligibility requirements, applying onshore is often possible.

**2. Is a skills assessment required for Subclass 186 Direct Entry?**

Yes. A positive skills assessment is mandatory for most occupations under the Direct Entry stream. Your qualifications and work experience must match Australian standards.

**3. How much work experience is required for Subclass 186 Direct Entry?**

Generally, at least 3 years of relevant work experience is required. This can include a combination of offshore and Australian experience, depending on your occupation.

**4. Can my employer sponsor me if I found the job myself?**

Yes. You can secure employment independently, and if the employer meets sponsorship requirements, they can nominate you for Subclass 186.

**5. How long does Subclass 186 Direct Entry processing take?**

Processing times vary depending on documentation, employer eligibility, and Department requests. Well-prepared applications typically move faster with fewer delays.
