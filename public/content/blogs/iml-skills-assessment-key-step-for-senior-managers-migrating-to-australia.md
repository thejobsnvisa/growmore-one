As we know, a skills assessment is one of the most important steps in Australia's skilled migration process. Based on your occupation, different assessing authorities are assigned to evaluate whether your experience and qualifications meet Australian standards. For senior management and leadership level roles, this responsibility falls to the Institute of Managers and Leaders (IML).

If you are planning to [apply for an Australia PR](https://www.growmore.one/how-do-i-apply-for-australia-pr-a-step-by-step-expert-guide/) as a Manager or Senior Executive, completing an IML skills assessment is a crucial requirement.


## What is the IML?

The Institute of Managers and Leaders Australia and New Zealand (IML) is the official assessing authority responsible for evaluating senior management professionals applying for Australian skilled migration.

This assessment is commonly required for major migration pathways, including:

- [Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189)](https://www.growmore.one/how-to-get-an-australian-pr-as-a-skilled-painting-trade-worker/)  
- [Skilled Nominated Visa (Subclass 190)](https://www.growmore.one/queensland-skilled-migration-program-2025-26-state-nomination-guide/)   
- Skilled Work Regional Visa (Subclass 491)  
- [Employer-Sponsored Visa](https://www.growmore.one/our-services/individual/employer-sponsored-visa/) (Subclass 186 – Direct Entry)

However, having a managerial job title alone is not enough. IML carefully reviews the actual duties performed, level of authority, and business impact.


## Confirm Your Assessing Authority Before You Apply

Before preparing your application, confirm that your occupation falls under IML’s scope. If not, your application may need to be assessed by a different authority.


## Understand How IML Assesses Your Profile

IML evaluates applicants based on:

- Leadership and strategic decision-making  
- Level of managerial responsibility  
- Organisational impact  
- Reporting structure  
- Career progression  

Applicants must demonstrate these through:

- Academic qualifications  
- Employment reference letters  
- Organisational charts  
- Position descriptions  
- Management Competency Framework responses  


## Prepare Supporting Documentation

A strong application requires clear and consistent documentation:

- Passport and identity documents  
- Updated CV  
- Academic qualifications and transcripts  
- Employment reference letters  
- Position descriptions  
- Two referees  
- Organisational charts  
- Management Competency Framework responses  


## IML Management Competency Framework Written Response

Applicants must complete written responses demonstrating leadership experience in:

- Managing and Leading Self  
- Managing and Leading Others  
- Managing and Leading the Business  

Each example should be based on real workplace situations and typically should not exceed 500 words.

Recommended format:

- Situation  
- Task  
- Action  
- Result  
- Reflection  


## Lodge the Application

Once all documents are ready, submit your application online through the IML portal.

Possible outcomes:

- Positive skills assessment  
- Negative outcome  
- Request for further information  

A positive assessment is generally valid for **3 years**.

## Key Considerations for a Strong Application

**Common issues include:**

- Overstating managerial responsibilities  
- Weak or generic reference letters  
- Misalignment between ANZSCO occupation and duties  
- Limited evidence of senior-level decision-making

## FAQs

### **1. What is the purpose of an IML Skills Assessment?**
It verifies whether your qualifications and leadership experience meet Australian standards.

### **2. Do I qualify just because I am a manager?**
No. IML evaluates actual responsibilities, not just job titles.

### **3. How long does it take?**
Around 10 weeks, with an express option available.

### **4. What if my application is incomplete?**
IML may request more information or reject the application.

### **5. Can I use it for PR?**
Yes. A positive assessment is valid for 3 years and can be used for PR pathways.
