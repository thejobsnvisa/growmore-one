## Which Australian Visa Is Right? 3 Questions to Decide
Every year, thousands of people plan to move to Australia, but the right visa makes all the difference. With over 100 visa subclasses available, each comes with unique eligibility criteria; choosing the right pathway becomes the key to a smooth journey.

The right visa can shape your future. A student on Subclass 500 can build a pathway toward permanent residency, a skilled worker on Subclass 190 may secure direct PR, and a partner visa applicant can reunite with their loved one without unnecessary delays. These outcomes aren’t the result of luck, but they come from informed decisions and proper planning.

So, if you’re looking for the perfect Australian visa, these three questions will clarify your options and guide your next steps.

**Question 1: What’s Your Primary Goal in Australia?**
------------------------------------------------------

First, you have to find an answer related to your goal to [visit Australia that helps in finding a particular visa type](https://www.growmore.one/australia-pr-visa-consultant/).

### **A) If Your Goal is Work / Skilled Migration**

If your goal is to work in Australia and eventually secure permanent residency, skilled migration and employer-sponsored visas are the most common pathways.

#### **Temporary Visa:**

*   [Skills in Demand Visa (Subclass 482)](https://www.growmore.one/a-complete-guide-for-skills-in-demand-visa-subclass-482/) – up to 4 years, employer-sponsored
*   Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494) – up to 5 years, employer-sponsored
*   Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 491) – live for 5 years in designated regional areas, apply for PR after 3 years

#### **Permanent Visa:**

*   [Employer Nomination Scheme (Subclass 186)](https://www.growmore.one/our-services/employers/standard-business-sponsorship/) – permanent residency with employer support
*   Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189) – points-based, no sponsorship needed
*   Skilled Nominated Visa (Subclass 190) – requires state nomination

Here, among employer-sponsored pathways (482, 186, 494) and skilled visas (190, 491, 189), the choice depends on whether you have an Australian employer ready to support you or if you prefer a points-based pathway.

**Note:** Most skilled visas require a formal skills assessment, which is done by specific authorities based on your occupation:

*   Engineers → Engineers Australia
*   IT professionals → Australian Computer Society (ACS)
*   Trades → Trades Recognition Australia (TRA)
*   General professions → VETASSESS
*   Accountants → CPA Australia
*   Surveyor → ISNSW

### **B) If Your Goal is Family Reunion or Partner Visa**

If your goal is to join your partner or family member in Australia, family-based visas may be suitable.

#### **Visa Types:**

*   **Partner Visa:** Subclass 820/801 (onshore), Subclass 309/100 (offshore)
*   **Parent Visa:** Subclass 804, Subclass 103, Subclass 870, sponsored by a child who is an Australian citizen/ PR holder
*   **Child visa:** Subclass 101, Subclass 802, sponsored by a parent who is an Australian citizen/ PR holder

**Requirements:** Evidence of a genuine relationship, sponsor eligibility, financial proof (for parent visas), and health checks.

In this type of visa, mainly missing documentation is the number one reason for delays or refusals.

### **C) If Your Goal is Tourism or Short Visits**

If you’re planning to visit Australia for tourism, a family visit, or short business purposes, a visitor visa may be suitable.

#### **Visa Types:**

*   [Visitor Visa (Subclass 600)](https://www.growmore.one/australian-visitor-visa-subclass-600-guide-expert-visa-agent-australia/) – up to 12 months
*   Electronic Travel Authority (ETA 601) – fast-track, AUD 20
*   eVisitor Visa (651) – for Europeans, free and fast

These visas are temporary and used for short-term travel.

### **D) If Your Goal is to Study**

If your goal is to study in Australia and, after that, build a long-term pathway, the student visa is the most suitable option.

*   **Visa Type:** Student Visa (Subclass 500)
*   **Duration:** Up to 6 years, depending on your course
*   **Work Rights:** Part-time up to 48 hours/week while classes are in session
*   **Post-study Opportunities:** Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485) lets you stay 2–4 years, depending on your qualification

**Question 2: Do You Meet the Eligibility Requirements?**
---------------------------------------------------------

Once you identify your goal, the next step is to check whether you meet visa eligibility requirements. Each visa type has specific criteria that must be satisfied before applying.

### **Key Eligibility Factors**

#### **1\. Age Requirements**

Most skilled visas require applicants to be under 45 years of age at the time of invitation. However, concessions may apply under certain pathways, such as employer-sponsored visas or [DAMA (Designated Area Migration Agreement) programs](https://www.growmore.one/our-services/dama/).

#### **2\. English Language Proficiency**

**Applicants may need to provide English test results from any of these authorities:**

*   IELTS/ PTE/ TOEFL/ Cambridge C1 Advanced/ LANGUAGECERT Academic/ MET/ CELPIP General

#### **3\. Work Experience**

Relevant work experience strengthens your application and improves your eligibility for skilled visas.

#### **4\. Educational Qualifications**

Your education must match your nominated occupation, particularly for skilled migration.

#### **5\. Health and Character Requirements**

Applicants must complete medical examinations and provide police clearance certificates for all types of visas.

#### **6\. Points Test (For Skilled Visas)**

Most skilled migration visas require a minimum of **65 points**, but higher points increase invitation chances.

**Question 3: Are You Looking for a Temporary Stay or Permanent Residency?**
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Your long-term goal also influences which visa pathway is best for you.

### **Temporary Visa Pathways**

**Some applicants start with temporary visas and later transition to permanent residency:**

*   Student Visa (500) → Temporary Graduate Visa (485) → Skilled Visa
*   Skills in Demand Visa (482) → Employer Nomination (186)
*   Regional Visa (491) → Permanent Visa (191)

This approach allows applicants to gain **Australian work experience**, which improves PR chances.

**FAQs**
--------

****Can I get an Australia PR without a job offer?****

Yes, you can apply for an Australia PR without a job offer through Subclass 189, 190, or 491. These are points-based visas and do not require employer sponsorship.

****Is PTE accepted for Australia PR?****

Yes, PTE Academic is accepted for Australia PR. Other accepted tests include IELTS, TOEFL, Cambridge English, etc.

****Can I get Australia PR after a student visa?****

Yes. A common pathway is Student Visa (500) → Temporary Graduate Visa (485) → Skilled Visa → PR. Australian work experience improves your PR chances.

****How to get Australia PR in 2026?****

You can apply for Australia PR through skilled migration visas such as Subclass 189, 190, or 491. The process typically includes a skills assessment, English test, points calculation, submitting an Expression of Interest (EOI), and applying after receiving an invitation.

****How long does Australia PR take in 2026?****

Processing times vary by visa type. Skilled visas may take 6 to 12 months or longer, depending on invitations, document checks, and application volume.
