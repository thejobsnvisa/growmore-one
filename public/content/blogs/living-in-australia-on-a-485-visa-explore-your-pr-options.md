## What is 485 Visa? 
 
The Subclass 485 visa is a temporary visa which is designed for international students who have recently completed an eligible qualification in Australia. It allows graduates to live, work, and gain practical experience in Australia after finishing their studies. 
 
In practical terms, the 485 visa acts as a bridge between student life and long-term migration pathways. 
 


## 485 Visa to Australia PR: Main Pathways 
 
Most 485 visa holders move toward PR through one of three structured migration streams: 

1. **Skilled Migration (Points-Tested Visas)**
2. **Employer-Sponsored Pathways**
3. **Regional Migration Options** 

**1. Skilled Migration Pathways**

This is one of the most common routes from a 485 visa to PR in Australia. General Skilled Migration can lead to PR based on your own skills without the need for employer sponsorship. Here are some options: 
 
* **Skilled Independent Visa 189:**
A points-tested permanent visa that does not require employer or state sponsorship. 
* **Skilled Nominated Visa 190:**
Requires nomination from an Australian state or territory. 
* **Skilled Work Regional Visa 491:** 
This is the provisional visa that requires regional nomination and can lead to Subclass 191 permanent residency. 
 
**Skilled Migration Is Based On:** * Occupation listed on relevant skilled occupation lists 
* Positive skills assessment 
* Points test (age, English, work experience, qualifications) 
* State or regional nomination (for 190 and 491) 
 
> **Here is the critical point:** Meeting minimum eligibility is not enough. You must remain competitive in the invitation system to get an invite. 
 
If you are thinking to go any of this pathway then during your 485 visa period, you should focus on: 
* Gaining employment aligned with your nominated occupation 
* Building skilled Australian work experience 
* Improving English scores if required 
* Preparing your skills assessment early 
* Monitoring state nomination criteria carefully 
 

**2. Employer-Sponsored Pathways (482 and 186)**
 
If you found an employer there who is ready to sponsor you then Employer-Sponsored Pathways are the best option. 
 
**Typical options include:** * **Subclass 482** – Temporary Skill Shortage (TSS) visa 
* **Subclass 186** – Employer Nomination Scheme (Permanent Visa) 
 
**Employer Sponsorship Requires:** 
 A genuine, full-time skilled role 
* An eligible occupation 
* An employer willing and eligible to sponsor 
* Meeting experience and English requirements 
* Strict compliance with migration legislation 
 
In this pathway, many times assumptions cause problems. If you believe that being a good employee would lead to sponsorship, you are mistaken. Because immigration authorities assess: 
* Whether your actual job duties align with the nominated occupation 
* Whether the position is genuine and ongoing 
* Whether you meet experience thresholds 
 
Even small inconsistencies between your duties and ANZSCO occupation description can result in refusal. 
 


**3. Regional Migration Pathways** 
 
If you are ok to live in regional area, then regional migration pathways offer some concessions. Potential advantages include: 
* Broader occupation lists 
* Lower points thresholds in some cases 
* Specific state and regional nomination streams 
 
However, not every job in a regional area meets state nomination requirements. States typically prioritise: 
* Occupations that are genuinely in local demand 
* Relevant regional work experience 
* Evidence of commitment to live and work in the region

## FAQs 
 
**1.What are the main Australia PR pathways after a 485 visa?**

 These are the main Australia PR pathways after a 485 visa: 
* Skilled Independent visa (subclass 189) 
* Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190) 
* Skilled Work Regional visa (subclass 491) 
* Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) 
* Employer Nomination Scheme (subclass 186) 

The right option depends on your occupation, experience, and competitiveness. 
 
**2.How many points are required for PR after a 485 visa?** 

The minimum eligibility for skilled migration is 65 points. However, invitation rounds often require higher scores based on occupation demand. You can calculate your points here: [https://www.growmore.one/points-calculator/](https://www.growmore.one/points-calculator/) 
 
**3.When should I start planning PR on a 485 visa?** 

You should start planning for PR immediately after your 485 visa is granted because waiting until the final year may reduce options and flexibility. If you feel any confusion about your eligibility or pathway, then seek advice from a registered migration agent in Australia. 
 
**4.Does Australian work experience improve PR chances after a 485 visa?** 

Yes, if you have relevant skilled Australian work experience, then it can increase your points score and strengthen state or employer nomination prospects. 
 
**5.Does an English score affect PR chances after a 485 visa?** 

Absolutely. If you have higher English scores, then it enhances your points and improves competitiveness for invitations. Even a small improvement in IELTS or PTE results can significantly strengthen your PR profile.
