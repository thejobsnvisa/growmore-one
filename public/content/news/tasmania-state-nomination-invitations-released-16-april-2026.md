## Tasmania Migration Update: State Nomination Invitations (16 April 2026)

Tasmania has released its latest state nomination invitation round on **16 April 2026**. The state has issued invitations under both **Subclass 190** and **Subclass 491** visa categories. As Tasmania enters the final quarter of the 2025–26 migration program year, the latest figures show a more selective and controlled invitation strategy.

For the 2025–26 program year, Tasmania received **1,850 nomination places** from the Australian Government. As allocations begin to narrow, each invitation round is becoming increasingly competitive for applicants currently in the Registration of Interest (ROI) pool.

## Tasmania Invitation Round: 16 April 2026

The Tasmania invitation round results show that the state issued **54 invitations** in this round, with an equal distribution across both visa subclasses.

| Visa Subclass | ROIs Invited | Lowest Score Invited | Pass Category |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Subclass 190** | 27 | 1000 | Gold Pass |
| **Subclass 491** | 27 | 60 Points | Orange-Plus Pass |
| **Total Invitations** | **54** | - | - |

### What This Indicates
* **Subclass 190** invitations were limited to Gold Pass candidates only.
* **Subclass 491** invitations remained competitive at 60 points.
* **Equal distribution** suggests balanced planning between permanent and regional migration pathways.

## Processing Timeline Update

Tasmania confirmed that the oldest nomination application waiting to be allocated to a case officer was lodged on: **22 January 2026**.

This suggests that processing is ongoing, though pending applications continue to build as invitations are issued weekly.


## Key Takeaways from This Round

* **Reduced Volume:** Tasmania issued 54 invitations in this round, a decrease compared to the previous round’s 78 invitations, indicating a more controlled allocation approach.
* **Selective Subclass 190:** Invitations for the 190 visa dropped significantly, with selections limited strictly to Gold Pass candidates.
* **Stable Subclass 491:** Invitations remained relatively stable, suggesting continued demand for regional workforce-aligned applicants.
* **Remaining Quota:** Tasmania still has **308 Subclass 190 places** and **349 Subclass 491 places** available from its total 1,850 nomination allocation.
* **Ongoing Rounds:** Weekly invitation rounds are continuing as Tasmania carefully manages its remaining quota in the final quarter of the program year.
