If you studied at a private training college in Australia in the past two years, this update may directly affect your qualification status.

The Australian Skills Quality Authority (ASQA) has cancelled the registration of Melbourne Training Centre Pty Ltd (RTO 45579), which operated under the trading name Studywise College. The cancellation took effect from 23 July 2025 and has been officially listed on the National Register. 

So, if your qualification falls within this period, you should check your status immediately, as it may affect its recognition for employment, skills assessment, or visa-related purposes.

## What Has Happened?

ASQA found serious compliance issues during its review of the provider. According to the regulator, the college:

* Failed to meet the required conditions of registration
* Issued qualifications and statements of attainment without proper assessment
* Did not ensure students had completed all required training before issuing certificates

Because of these findings, ASQA has raised concerns about the validity of qualifications issued by this provider.

## Who Is Affected?

ASQA has confirmed that it will now review qualifications and statements of attainment issued between 1 May 2023 to 23 July 2025.

Here are the qualifications issued during the review period and under scrutiny:

|     |     |
| --- | --- |
| **Code** | **Qualification Title** |
| CHC33015 | Certificate III in Individual Support |
| CHC33021 | Certificate III in Individual Support |
| CHC52021 | Diploma of Community Services |
| CHC43121 | Certificate IV in Disability Support |


## What Happens Next for Students?

ASQA is beginning a formal process to assess impacted qualifications. As part of this:

Students may receive a Notice of Intent to Cancel Qualification 

Affected individuals will be given a chance to respond in writing 

A strict deadline will be provided in each notice (usually a specific date and time) 

Final decisions will be made after reviewing each individual response 

If no response is submitted within the deadline, ASQA may proceed with cancellation based on available evidence.


## How This Matters for Migration and Careers

This goes beyond education compliance and may impact your job opportunities, skills assessment, and Australia PR pathways if qualifications are reviewed or challenged.

This can potentially affect:

Skills assessment applications, where the validity of qualifications is a core requirement 

Skilled visa eligibility, especially where qualification points or occupation criteria are involved 

Employer verification checks, where the authenticity of training is independently confirmed 

Job offers linked to Australian qualifications, particularly in regulated or licensed occupations

If you studied at this provider during the affected period, do not ignore any communication from ASQA. Check your qualification records carefully and respond within the given deadline if you receive a notice.

For those who are unsure about their eligibility, contact a MARA registered agent Australia immediately for a detailed case assessment before taking any further steps.
