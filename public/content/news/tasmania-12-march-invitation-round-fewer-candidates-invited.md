## Tasmania 12 March Invitation Round: Fewer Candidates Invited

Tasmania has conducted its latest state nomination invitation round this week as part of its weekly migration cycle.

However, this round has drawn attention for two key reasons:  
- A noticeably smaller number of invitations compared to earlier rounds  
- Subclass 190 invitations being issued only to Gold Pass candidates  

This indicates that Tasmania is currently taking a more selective approach while managing its remaining nomination places and existing applications.


## Invitations Issued in the Latest Round

According to the latest round on **12 March 2026**, Tasmania issued **33 ROIs** across:

- **Subclass 190 (Skilled Nominated Visa):** 14 invitations  
- **Subclass 491 (Skilled Work Regional Visa):** 19 invitations  

### Lowest Selection Levels

- **Subclass 190:** Only Gold Pass candidates invited  
- **Subclass 491:** Minimum 40 points (Orange Pass)  

Subclass 190 invitations were granted only to top-priority applicants, showing a strong preference for high-ranking candidates.


## Current Pipeline of Applications

Tasmania’s migration program continues to handle a significant number of applications.

| Category | Subclass 190 | Subclass 491 |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| ROIs on hand | 471 | 196 |
| Applications lodged (pending) | 394 | 214 |
| Invitations issued (not yet lodged) | 68 | 59 |

- A large number of ROIs are still waiting in the system  
- Hundreds of applications are under assessment  
- The state must balance new invitations with ongoing processing  


## Nomination Places Still Available

Tasmania has shared remaining nomination places:

- **Subclass 190:** 558 places  
- **Subclass 491:** 424 places  

While places are still available, invitations will now be issued more carefully.

The oldest pending application dates back to **12 December 2025**.


## What This Means for Skilled Migration Applicants

- Weekly invitation rounds continue, but competition is strong  
- Subclass 190 invitations are limited to top candidates  
- Pending applications may affect future invitation numbers
