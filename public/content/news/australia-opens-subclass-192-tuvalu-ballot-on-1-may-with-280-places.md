Good news for Tuvalu citizens who are considering a move to Australia. The Department of Home Affairs has announced the opening of the **[Pacific Engagement Visa (Subclass 192) – Treaty Stream](https://immi.homeaffairs.gov.au/visas/getting-a-visa/visa-listing/pacific-engagement-visa-192)** ballot from **1 May 2026 to 1 June 2026**. A total of **280 Treaty stream visa places** are available for the 2026–27 program year.

This **Falepili Mobility Pathway** offers eligible Tuvalu citizens an opportunity to become permanent residents of Australia through a **random ballot selection process**.

Unlike many other Australian migration pathways, the Subclass 192 Treaty Stream provides significant flexibility and allows successful applicants to:

- Live, work, and study anywhere in Australia  
- No minimum skill level requirements  
- No occupation restrictions  
- No English language requirements  

## How the Subclass 192 Tuvalu Ballot Works

The process involves two main steps:

### Step 1: Ballot Registration
Eligible Tuvalu citizens must first register for the ballot between **1 May 2026 and 1 June 2026**.

### Step 2: Random Selection
After the ballot closes, the **Department of Home Affairs** will randomly select applicants, who will then be invited to submit a visa application.

The selection period is expected shortly after the ballot closes.

## Eligibility Requirements

To register for the **Subclass 192 Treaty Stream (Tuvalu)** ballot, applicants must:

- Be at least **18 years old** when the ballot opens  
- Be a **citizen of Tuvalu** (not obtained through investment)  
- Hold a **valid Tuvalu passport**  
- Be born in Tuvalu or have a parent/grandparent born in Tuvalu  
- Not be a **New Zealand citizen**  
- Not already registered in the same ballot  
- Pay the **AUD 25 ballot registration fee**

Applicants can register **from inside or outside Australia**.

## Why This Visa Stands Out

The **Pacific Engagement Visa (Subclass 192)** stands out because it offers an alternative to skilled and employer-sponsored visas, with:

- Direct **permanent residency (PR)**  
- No occupation restrictions  
- No employer sponsorship required  
- No English test requirements  
- No points-based competition  

However, with only **280 places available**, early preparation and accurate registration are critical.

## What This Means for Applicants

This visa works like a rare **Australia PR lottery**.

If selected, you may receive a direct opportunity to apply for permanent residency through a random draw. However, applicants must still meet all eligibility criteria and be ready to lodge their visa application within the given timeframe.
