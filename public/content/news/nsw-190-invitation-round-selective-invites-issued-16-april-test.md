# NSW Issues Selective 190 Invitations for Key Occupations on 16 April 2026

As announced earlier by New South Wales, the state has conducted another **Subclass 190 Skilled Nominated Visa** invitation round on **16 April 2026**, issuing invitations across a range of professional, trade, and healthcare occupations.

This latest round indicates that NSW continues to prioritise **onshore candidates**, with all reported invitations issued to applicants currently residing in Australia. This aligns with the state’s recent trend of targeting candidates already contributing to the local workforce.

## Key Takeaways From This Round

- The round highlights a **wide variation in points requirements**, ranging from **65 to 100 points**.
- Trade occupations such as **Painter** and **Carpenter & Joiner** received invitations at lower points, indicating strong demand in construction and infrastructure sectors.
- Healthcare roles like **Resident Medical Officer** and **Registered Nurse (Critical Care & Emergency)** continue to be in demand due to workforce shortages.
- Professional occupations such as **Analyst Programmer** and **Food Technologist** required higher points (100), reflecting increased competition.
- Points were **not the only deciding factor**—some candidates received invitations even without working in their nominated occupation.
- NSW appears to be focusing on **overall workforce needs rather than just occupation alignment**.

## Final Insight

The latest NSW invitation round shows that **Australia PR opportunities are still available**, but the selection process is becoming more **targeted and competitive**. A strong profile and the right strategy can significantly improve your chances of receiving a nomination.
