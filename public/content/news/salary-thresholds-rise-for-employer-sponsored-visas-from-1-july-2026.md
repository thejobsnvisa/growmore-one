## Salary Thresholds Rise for Employer-Sponsored Visas from 1 July 2026

If you are an employer sponsoring skilled workers or a professional planning to apply for an Australian visa, the upcoming salary threshold changes will affect you directly.

Australia is set to increase the minimum salary requirements for employer-sponsored visas. These changes are based on the November 2025 AWOTE figures released by the Australian Bureau of Statistics and will take effect from **1 July 2026**.


## What’s Changing?

**The updated salary thresholds are:**

- Core Skills Income Threshold (CSIT): $79,499 (up from $76,515)  
- Specialist Skills Income Threshold (SSIT): $146,717 (up from $141,210)

**These thresholds apply to:**

- **Subclass 482** – Core & Specialist streams  
- **Subclass 186** – Temporary Residence Transition (TRT) & Direct Entry  
- **Labour Agreement streams** under Subclass 482 and 186  

However, Subclass 494 and Subclass 187 TRT visas are not automatically updated because they are tied to TSMIT.


## Critical Details for Sponsoring Skilled Workers

- **Meet deadlines:** Applications before 1 July 2026 follow old thresholds  
- **Plan salaries carefully:** Only base salary counts (no bonuses or super)  
- **Budget properly:** Increased costs impact hiring strategy  
- **Prepare documents early:** Avoid delays and rejections  


## Practical Steps for Employers

- Complete the required **4-week advertising period early**  
- Lodge applications before July 1 to avoid higher thresholds  
- Get expert advice to ensure compliance  


So, if you’re preparing employer-sponsored visa applications and these changes are causing confusion, it’s best to seek expert guidance to ensure all salary thresholds are met correctly.
