Not every Australian visa is designed for employment, but some are also considered to build skills, improve experience, and prepare you for future opportunities, and subclass 407 is one of them.

This visa allows individuals to improve their skills in their occupation, field of study, or professional expertise by participating in workplace training in Australia. While it is a temporary visa, it plays an important role in strengthening your career and future migration options.

So, if you are looking to apply for this training visa but do not know about how this visa works, who it's for, and eligibility requirements, this guide will walk you through everything.

## What is the Subclass 407 Training Visa?

The [Subclass 407 Training Visa](https://www.growmore.one/new-rule-407-training-visa-applications-allowed-only-after-nomination-approval/) is a temporary visa that allows individuals to take part in workplace-based occupational training activities that happen in Australia. 

This visa helps you improve your skills for a job, gain experience in an area of tertiary study, and also advance your expertise in professional development programs. It is not designed for ongoing employment. Instead, it focuses on structured training programs that help applicants build skills and industry exposure in Australia.

## What Can You Do on a Subclass 407 Visa?

With this visa, you can:

* Participate in workplace-based training
* Improve your skills in your current occupation
* Gain Australian workplace exposure
* Complete professional development programs
* Travel in and out of Australia while your visa is valid
* Include eligible family members in your application

## Types of Training Under Subclass 407 Visa

There are three types of occupational training available under the **Subclass 407 visa**:

### 1. Occupational Training Required for Registration

This type is for professionals who must complete training to become registered, licensed, or certified in their occupation. The training is workplace-based and is required to legally work either in Australia or your home country. This option is suitable when the training is mandatory, not just for skill improvement.

### 2. Occupational Training to Improve Skills in an Eligible Occupation

This is the most common Subclass 407 training type. It is designed for individuals who already have some experience but want to improve their skills through structured workplace training in Australia. 
To qualify, you must:
* Be nominated in an eligible skilled occupation
* Have at least 12 months of relevant experience
* Experience must be within the last 24 months
* Training must be structured and tailored to your skills

### 3. Occupational Training for Capacity Building Overseas

This type focuses on building skills that benefit your home country. It includes:
* **Overseas Qualification:** For students studying overseas who need practical training in Australia to complete their qualification (usually up to 6 months).
* **Government-Supported Training:** Training supported by an Australian government agency or the home country government.
* **Professional Development:** Structured classroom-based programs in Australia for overseas professionals or managers sent by their employers.

## Sponsorship and Nomination Requirements

One of the most important parts of the [Subclass 407 visa](https://www.growmore.one/new-rule-407-training-visa-applications-allowed-only-after-nomination-approval/) is sponsorship. To apply for this, applicants must have an approved sponsor, the sponsor must submit a nomination, and the nomination must be approved before you apply for a visa. 

This means you cannot apply independently for this visa. Your sponsor must be approved as a Temporary Activities Sponsor before the visa application is submitted.

### Eligibility Requirements for Subclass 407 Visa

To apply for this visa, you must:
* Have an approved sponsor
* Have an approved nomination
* Usually be 18 years or older
* Have functional English
* Meet health requirements
* Meet character requirements
* Have adequate health insurance
* Be a genuine temporary entrant


## Important Considerations

### Can You Include Family Members?

Yes, you can include family members such as spouse/partner or dependent children under this subclass 407. Your family members must also meet health and character requirements. Additionally, your dependent can also apply later as a subsequent entrant.

### Can You Change Sponsor?

Yes, you can change your sponsor while on a Subclass 407 visa, but there are a few important things to understand. If you decide to continue your training with a different organisation, the new organisation must first become your sponsor and submit a new nomination for your training. Once the new nomination is approved, you can continue your occupational training with the new sponsor for the remaining time on your current visa.

### Does A Subclass 407 Visa Lead to an Australia PR?

The Subclass 407 visa does not directly lead to permanent residency, but it can help strengthen your future migration opportunities. So, if your long-term goal is an [Australia PR](https://www.growmore.one/), then you can later move to employer-sponsored visas or skilled migration visas. These pathways depend on your occupation, experience, and the opportunities you gain during your training in Australia.

## FAQs

1. **Is the Subclass 407 visa a work visa?**
   No, the Subclass 407 visa is not a full work visa. It is designed for training purposes, and any work you do must be part of your approved training program.

2. **How long can I stay in Australia on a Subclass 407 visa?**
   You can usually stay in Australia for up to 2 years, depending on the length of your approved training program.

3. **Do I need work experience to apply for Subclass 407?**
   Yes, in most cases, you need at least 12 months of relevant work experience in your occupation within the last 24 months.

4. **Can my family join me on a Subclass 407 visa?**
   Yes, you can include your spouse/partner and dependent children in your application, or they can apply later as subsequent entrants.

5. **Can the Subclass 407 visa help with future PR options?**
   While the Subclass 407 visa does not directly lead to PR, it can help you gain Australian experience and improve your profile for future visa pathways.
