
If you’re planning to apply for an Australian Parent visa, there’s an important update you should be aware of.

From **22 April 2026**, paper-based applications for the four permanent Parent visa subclasses will transition to **online lodgement through ImmiAccount**, as the Department of Home Affairs rolls out an updated digital system.

In simple terms, Australia is shifting Parent visa applications away from paperwork and moving them into a fully online process.

---

## What’s Changing?

Starting 22 April 2026, the relevant Parent visa webpages will be updated with clear instructions on how to apply online using ImmiAccount. This information will be available under the **“Step by Step”** tab for each visa subclass, making the process more structured and easier to follow.

Instead of preparing physical documents and sending paper applications, applicants will now be guided through an online system where everything is uploaded and managed digitally.

However, paper applications will only be considered in **exceptional circumstances**, such as verified issues with ImmiAccount. In such cases, the issue must be confirmed by the **eServices Support Helpdesk** through the official ImmiAccount Technical Support form.

---

## Parent Visa Subclasses Affected

This change applies to the following four permanent Parent visa subclasses:

- **Subclass 103 – Parent Visa**
- **Subclass 804 – Aged Parent Visa**
- **Subclass 143 – Contributory Parent Visa**
- **Subclass 864 – Contributory Aged Parent Visa**

---

## Why This Matters

This shift is not just a technical update—it directly affects how smoothly your application is handled.

Online lodgement is expected to make the process:

- More organised and less paperwork-heavy  
- Easier to track at every stage  
- Faster in terms of communication and updates  
- Less prone to delays (no physical document handling)

---

## What You Should Do Now

If you are already preparing a paper application, it may be worth **pausing and reassessing your timing**.

Once the online system is live, it will likely be a more practical option compared to submitting paper forms just before the transition.

In short, applying online after **22 April 2026** could save you time and reduce unnecessary hassle.
