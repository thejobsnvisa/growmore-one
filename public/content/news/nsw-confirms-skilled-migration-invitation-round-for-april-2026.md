## NSW Confirms Skilled Migration Invitation Round for April 2026
If you’re aiming for NSW state nomination, then the time has come to keep all your documents and Expression of Interest (EOI) up to date, because the state has confirmed a date for the upcoming nomination round for Subclass 190 and Subclass 491 visas.

These rounds will provide skilled professionals the opportunity to secure state nomination and move closer to Australian permanent residency.

**Skilled Nominated Visa (Subclass 190) – April 2026 Round**
------------------------------------------------------------

The Subclass 190 invitation round is scheduled for the week commencing **13 April 2026.**

*   **EOI Update Deadline**: COB Sunday, 12 April 2026

**Skilled Work Regional Visa (Subclass 491) – Pathway 2 Round**
---------------------------------------------------------------

The Subclass 491 Pathway 2 invitation round will follow in the week **commencing 27 April 2026.**

*   **EOI Update Deadline**: COB Sunday, 26 April 2026

**Key Considerations for Applicants**
-------------------------------------

*   NSW has confirmed that both the upcoming NSW PR invitation rounds will be highly competitive, and only occupations listed on the NSW Skilled Occupation List will be considered.
*   Invitations will be issued based on the most current EOI information. So, ensure your points, work experience, and documentation are fully updated.
*   Applicants should also ensure that their nominated occupation aligns with NSW’s regional or state-specific requirements for each visa subclass.
*   Meeting minimum points does not guarantee an invitation. Here, higher points and competitive profiles have a stronger chance.

**Action Steps for Aspirants**
------------------------------

1.  **Update your EOI immediately** before the deadline to reflect your latest qualifications, work experience, and points.
2.  **Prepare supporting documents** such as skills assessments, English test results, and employment references in advance.
3.  **Check occupation eligibility** on the NSW Skilled Occupation List for your visa subclass.
4.  **Monitor NSW updates** regularly to avoid missing any sudden changes or additional rounds.
5.  **Consult a** **skilled migration consultant in Australia** to audit your profile, maximise your points, and ensure your application is fully competitive.
